Chapter 7

Ghosts in the Code

Caine: Ladies, gentlemen… Zooble… WELCOME to the Verti-Go Gauntlet!

Caine motions to the towering structure.

Caine: A perilous test of tenacity, terror… and tenuous ties to safety regulations.

Ragatha: That’s… really high.

Daisy: It’s just like the jungle gym!

Pomni: It’s nothing like the jungle gym, Dee.

Daisy: There’s platforms. There’s bars. You climb. You don’t die. Same thing!

Pomni: That was two stories high over foam. This is… how many stories is this?

Jax: Kid’s got a point, though. It’s a taller jungle gym.

Pomni: You are not helping.

Daisy jumps in place.

Daisy: I can do it.

Pomni: Absolutely not. You’ll fall.

Daisy: You’d let him do it.

She motions at Jax.

Jax: I’m a trained professional.

Zooble: At making bad decisions.

Daisy: I can do bad decisions!

Pomni: That’s not the selling point you think it is.

Daisy: I climb higher junk all the time. You just don’t look up.

Pomni: When have you…?

Jax: She’s got you there, Pom.

Pomni: You knew about it?

Jax: Relax. She’s not five anymore.

Pomni: Jax!

Daisy: I’m not gonna stand on the sidelines while you guys have fun.

Pomni groans, then sighs.

Pomni: Fine. Be careful, Dee.

Jax puts his hand on Daisy’s shoulder.

Jax: You’ll go slow. No stunts. I mean it, I know you… You’re literally my child.

Daisy: Okay, Dad.

Ragatha: She’s doing really well.

Jax: That’s my gremlin.

Ragatha: Don’t call her that.

Pomni: I can’t look. Is she dying?

Daisy: I’m not dying!

Jax: She’s not dying, Pom.

Pomni peeks between her fingers.

Pomni: This is so unsafe…

Jax: Welcome to my world.

Daisy takes a more dramatic jump than necessary.

Pomni: Daisy!

Daisy: I had it!

Pomni turns to Ragatha.

Pomni: That child will give me a heart attack.

Ragatha: You can have yours after I’ve had mine.

Jax: Ladies, she’s doing fine!

Zooble: I gotta agree with bunny-boy here. She’s got it.

Jax: Thank you, Zoobie.

Zooble: Don’t make me regret it.

Daisy reaches a narrow beam.

Daisy: Okay… this one’s tall.

Pomni: You can come down if you want, Dee!

Daisy: I don’t want!

She inches onto the beam.

Daisy: See? I’ve got good balance.

Pomni and Jax say You get that from me. at the same time.

Jax: No, she didn’t.

Pomni: Absolutely not.

Daisy grins, takes three quick steps, then jumps onto the next platform.

Jax: Show-off.

Pomni: That’s your fault.

She’s almost at the top.

Pomni: Last one, Dee. Then you come down.

Daisy: One more, Mom. Chill.

The last gap is wider than the others. The platform swings softly on chains.

Daisy eyes it.

Daisy: Okay… easy…

Pomni: Don’t.

She takes three steps back.

Jax: Daisy…

Pomni: Don’t you dare!

She runs.

For a second, she’s flying.

Halfway through, she realizes she’s a bit short. The platform seems too far.

Daisy: Oh no.

Her stomach drops.

Daisy: FUCK

The platform glitches. It lurches forward toward her slightly. Just enough. Barely noticeable from below.

Daisy’s hands slap down on the hard wood, which shouldn’t be there yet. She hits hard, chest slams against the edge. Claws dig into the wood.

Daisy: Ouch.

Another glitch. It snaps back to where it was.

Daisy hauls herself up, shaking.

Gangle: Did… did that move?

Zooble: I’m ninety percent sure that should have been a splat.

Pomni: Daisy! Are you okay?

Daisy: I’m… fine.

Pomni: You almost fell!

Daisy: But I didn’t.

Pomni: That’s not the point!

Zooble turns to Caine.

Zooble: Caine, did you catch her?

Caine: I certainly did not! Where would be the excitement in that?

Daisy gets back on her feet. She brushes herself off.

Daisy: See? Jungle gym.

Pomni stares at the dangling platforms, at the spot where the world cheated.

Pomni: It is nothing like it, Dee.

Daisy glances back at the gap.

She still feels it. The angle, the distance… she shouldn’t have made it.

She shoves the thought away.

Daisy: I… thought I could do it.

Jax: Solid work. Never do that again.

Daisy: You would.

Jax: Yeah, but that’s my thing.

Daisy snorts.

Daisy: Maybe it’s mine too.

She starts climbing down, carefully this time.

The others wait at the bottom. Pomni vibrates in place.

Daisy drops the last few feet. Pomni’s on her immediately.

Pomni: Daisy!

Pomni grabs her shoulders and pulls her into a slightly too tight hug.

Pomni: What were you thinking?

Daisy: I said I had it.

Pomni: You did not have it. You almost fell.

Pomni pulls back to look at her.

Pomni: Are you alright? Does anything feel wrong? Your head? Your ribs? Your…

Daisy: Mom, I’m fine!

Daisy shrugs her hand off. Not hard, just enough.

Daisy: I just need… I’m tired, okay? Could we not make this a big thing?

Pomni opens her mouth, then closes it.

Pomni: We’re… talking about this later.

Daisy: Whatever.

She turns away.

Pomni: Daisy.

Daisy: I’m going to my room.

She doesn’t look back.

Daisy shuts her door with her foot and lets herself fall backwards onto her bed. She stares at the ceiling.

Daisy: Ow.

She keeps replaying the jump in her mind.

The run. The jump. The empty space below her.

She squeezes her eyes shut.

She sees the gap again. The platform. The way it… glitched.

She throws an arm over her eyes.

Daisy: It was Caine. He does that all the time. He messes with stuff, that’s his whole thing.

She doesn’t believe herself. She’s seen it before. Today just felt… bigger.

The walls glitch. She’s used to it now. It comes randomly.

She sits up. Pulls her knees to her chest.

Daisy: I’m okay. I did it. I didn’t fall. Everyone saw I didn’t fall.

Her chest still aches where it hit the edge.

She swings her legs off the bed. She sighs.

Daisy: You’re such a mess, Dee. You should go apologize to Mom.

She gets up, opens her door, and slips into the hallway.

Pomni’s room is right next door. She approaches.

She hears her and Jax inside. She can’t quite make out what they’re saying.

The door is slightly ajar. She should just knock, walk in, and tell her everything’s fine.

She raises her fist to knock.

Pomni: …Daisy…

She stops herself.

She pushes her back against the wall and turns her ears toward the small gap in the door.

She didn’t come here for this, but they’re talking about her.

Jax: I’m just saying, if I thought I’d be a goner and suddenly caught myself, I’d need a lie-down too.

Jax’s voice is muffled. Daisy presses her shoulder to the wall.

Jax: Kid stuck the landing, though. Ten out of ten stupid, very on-brand. Did you hear her? She used a swear! I think Zooble’s been teaching her phonetically.

Pomni: This isn’t funny.

Jax: I didn’t say it was.

Pomni: She scared the SHIT out of me.

Jax: She’s okay, Pom. She scared you, sure, but she’s okay.

Pomni: She almost fell, Jax!

Jax: And she didn’t.

Pomni: Cause the platform glitched.

Jax: Right.

Pomni: Did you see it? That wasn’t Caine.

Jax: You’re saying…

Pomni: It was our daughter, Jax.

Daisy covers her mouth with her paw.

Jax: You’re saying she… what? She caught herself with it?

Pomni: Not intentionally, I don’t think. She just… made it happen.

Jax: You’re overthinking it.

Pomni: I’m really not…

Jax: Pom…

They fall silent for a second.

Pomni: Do you… remember Gummigoo?

Jax: The gummy alligator with the Australian accent? Yeah. You’re always weird whenever he shows up.

Pomni: No, I mean the first time we met him.

Jax: Yeah, you tried to bring him back to the circus. Didn’t last.

Pomni: Jax…

Jax: Are you telling me you have feelings for him?

Pomni: What? No! I… maybe, but… he was my friend.

Jax: The… first one?

Pomni: Yeah. Remember when I disappeared with him, and I showed up later in a flying truck?

Jax: Very disappointing. What happened to you two?

Pomni: We clipped through the map. Fell into some… debug zone.

Jax: Oh, SHIT.

Pomni: The place was… empty. Except… lots of teapots for some reason. And in one room, there was all these character models… t-posing.

Jax: Asserting dominance. Go on.

Pomni: Gummigoo was there, just… staring at himself.

Jax: What does that have to do with Daisy?

Pomni: She’s starting to ask the same kind of questions.

Jax: What questions?

Pomni: What am I? What happens to me when the adventure ends?

Jax: And so…

Pomni: I told him about the circus. How maybe… he could be somebody real here.

Jax: Worked out great.

Pomni: Then Caine just… Now every time I see him, it’s like he doesn’t remember me. He’s a fresh copy.

Pomni sobs.

Jax: Why didn’t you tell me about it?

Pomni: I tried. But every time I try to bring it up, you just dismiss it. Say it’s just an NPC.

Jax: I’m sorry Pomni. I didn’t know…

Pomni: And now I’m worried about Daisy. About… if she finds out what she is.

Jax: Then what? We just lie to her until she figures it out and hates us?

Pomni: I don’t know, Jax. I don’t have any other plans.

Daisy can’t bear to hear anymore. She rushes back to her room.

Jax’s ears perk up.

Daisy lies on her bed, staring at the ceiling.

Daisy: NPC. Gummy guy. Fresh copy… what are they on about?

She turns to her side, staring at the wall instead.

Daisy: NPC… Non… something. Non-person?

Her stomach twists.

The walls flicker briefly.

Daisy: Stop it.

She presses her eyes shut.

Daisy: He… saw himself. Like in my nightmare. But Mom said this was during an adventure. He wasn’t dreaming.

She rolls back on her back. She covers her eyes with her arm.

Daisy: What does it mean? What does any of it mean?

The walls flicker again.

A knock at the door. The flicker stops.

Pomni: Dee? Can I come in?

Daisy gives no answer. She curls on her side, back to the door, blanket up to her chin.

The door cracks open.

Pomni: Daisy. Are… you okay?

Daisy: I’m fine.

Pomni slips in. She sits on the edge of the bed.

Pomni: You went straight to your room earlier.

Daisy: Yeah.

Pomni: You scared me, you know.

Daisy: I didn’t fall. I’m fine.

Pomni: That’s not…

Pomni puts her hand on Daisy’s shoulder.

Daisy turns further. Pomni’s hand falls onto the blanket.

Daisy: I’m not a baby. I can handle things.

Pomni: I know you’re not a baby.

Daisy: You don’t act like it.

Pomni flinches.

Pomni: I act like a parent who’s scared for her child.

Daisy shrugs under the blanket.

Pomni: The circus… sometimes does weird stuff. That’s just… Caine being Caine.

Daisy’s ear twitches.

Daisy: Right. Caine.

Pomni: Yeah.

Daisy stares at the wall.

Daisy: What if… something was… different, with someone… would you tell them?

Pomni freezes.

Pomni: Different how?

Daisy: I don’t know. Nevermind.

Pomni goes silent.

Pomni: There’s nothing wrong with you, Dee.

Daisy: You sure?

Pomni: Yes.

Daisy pulls the blanket higher.

Daisy: Can we… not do this right now? I’m tired.

Pomni reaches for her daughter’s shoulder, but stops herself. She exhales.

Pomni: You know you can talk to me… right?

Daisy: Yep.

Pomni gets up. She walks over to the door.

Pomni: I love you, Dee.

No answer.

Pomni waits a moment. She desperately wants there to be one.

She quietly slips out, tears welling in her eyes.

Daisy flips back onto her back.

Daisy: Ugh!

The door latch glitches itself locked.

Daisy’s eyes start getting wet.

Daisy: What… am I?
